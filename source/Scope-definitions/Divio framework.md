# **What is the Divio framework**

The Divio framework is a way of categorizing documentation into different types of writing, where each type has a distinct job to do.

## **Categories**

The framework defines four categories:

* **Tutorials:**  
  Learning-oriented content that guides users through a series of steps to complete a project. Unlike how-to guides, tutorials provide context and explanation along the way – the user may not know exactly what they want to achieve before they start, but will have learned something concrete by the end.  
  **Note:** Tutorials may reference how-to guides for specific steps.  
* **How-to guides:**  
  Problem-oriented content that takes users through the steps required to solve a specific task. The user already knows what they want to achieve and is looking for the answer to 'How do I...?'. How-to guides do not explain concepts – if background is needed, link to an explanation instead. They do not need to start from the beginning or cover everything, just enough to get the user to their goal. Titles should clearly reflect what the guide does, e.g., How to deploy a smart contract module.  
* **Explanations:**  
  Understanding-oriented discussions that clarify and illuminate a particular topic. They provide context, background, and broader perspective – they do not instruct the reader in how to do something. A topic for an explanation is not defined by a task to achieve or a learning goal, but by what makes sense to cover as a coherent subject area.  
* **References:**  
  Information-oriented technical descriptions of systems, tools, and features. The only job of reference material is to describe – accurately and completely. It does not explain concepts or instruct the reader in how to achieve tasks. It may include basic description of how to use something – for example how to call a function or use an API endpoint – but this is description, not instruction.

Note: In our site structure any document type may be structured as a parent document with sub-documents when the scope is sufficiently complex or covers multiple stages. This is especially the case for tutorials and explanations.

## **Goal**

The goal is to make better documentation: Most documentation problems don’t come from lack of effort, but from mixing these types together. When each type is kept separate and distinct, documentation becomes easier to write, maintain, and use.

Concordium has adopted this framework to ensure our documentation consistently meets the needs of our readers – giving them the right type of content at the right time.
